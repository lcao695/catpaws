    Studio Work

    Projects completed for the "Visual Literacy and Design" studio with Professor Jack Elliott. Click each project to learn more.

    "A/propos: understanding 3D form through the power of subtraction and hidden geometries." Museum board, water-based glue. Polyhedron made from the geometric parti of an archimedean hexagonal tessellation.

    "Tessellas: investigating transformations from the two-dimensional to the three-dimensional realm." A 3D object made from various extrusions of a 2D tessellating pattern, specifically a hexagonal Archimedean tessellation.

    "Tectonics: a consideration of the structure and poetics of three-dimensional expression and visual phenomena." A curved truss bridge weighing 28 grams, spanning 24 inches and withstanding >3000 grams of pressure.

    "Covering Style: examination of visual expression technique through elements and syntax as an investigation into form and content." A hand-painted book cover expressing Irish architect and designer Eileen Gray's style through the use of graphic motif, geometry, and typography.

    Individual Ceramics Exploration 2023

    Researched clay bodies and clay-working methodologies to produce teapots after three weeks of ceramic-working experience.

    Haven Exhibition

    This project was an exercise in creating a couture line for a specific audience and then designing an exhibition space to display that couture line. The brand "Haven" is timeless and for women of all ages who want a mixture of classy and carefree playfulness in their daily lives.

    I drafted my design for this exhibition using Revit, but ultimately rendered the visualization using the Electronic Arts video game "The Sims 4."
